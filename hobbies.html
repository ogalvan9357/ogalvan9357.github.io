Rockclimbing

I go indoor rockclimbing once or sometimes twice a week at Pacific Pipe, a gym in Oakland. In the past, I was never really interested in sports or traditional gyms but I actually really enjoy rockclimbing. There are three different types of indoor rockclimbing: Belay, Bouldering, and Lead. I do both Belay and Bouldering.

Belay involves wearing a harness and hooking into a rope that will catch you when you fall. It requires a partner to manage the rope with a Belay device. These walls are very tall since you can fall being connected to the rope.

Bouldering does not involve ropes, harnesses, or partners. These are much shorter, but usually more difficult walls that people free climb. The walls are short so that you don't fall so far. The ground is also much more cushioned.

Lead is the most difficult form of rockclimbing. People usually master Lead to then go outdoor rockclimbing. Lead requires a harness and partner but the rope does not hold your weight from above you. You must continually hook into clips on your way up. It is your partners weight prevents you from falling, rather than a Belay device.
